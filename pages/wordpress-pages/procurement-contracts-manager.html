We’re looking for a Procurement & Contracts Manager to develop and manage procurement and contract management for the California Office of Digital Innovation (ODI). In this role, you’ll be responsible for the continued development and implementation of a full-service procurement and contracting program within ODI including the supervision and mentorship of Associate Governmental Program Analysts. As a procurement and contracts expert, you will be responsible for improving the efficacy of the contract, procurement, and facilities management functions to deliver transparency, cost savings, risk minimization, and better purchasing strategies for ODI. This role requires strong organizational skills that include creating filing systems that are easy for others to navigate. You have strong interpersonal skills and are good at building relationships and educating others on processes, policies, and best practices related to procurement and contracting. You love collaborating with others and are open to feedback; always looking for ways to improve current processes and bring creative solutions to the table.

Location: ODI staff reside throughout California. This position provides telework opportunities in accordance with agency telework policies. (Must currently reside in California)

Annual Salary: $76,836 to $95,448 based on experience

Benefits: The state offers many benefits

What you’ll do

  • Serves as ODI’s Procurement Administrator and Subject Matter Expert (SME) on Procurement. Acts as the Small Business, Micro Business and DVBE Advocate.
  • Responsible for ODI’s procurement files and ensures that all purchases abide by State policy. 
  • Researches and develops internal policy, procedures and guidelines regarding ODI Procurement and Contracting practices.  
  • Analyzes and implements complex changes in state laws, rules and regulations pertaining to procurement. Consults with management on how changes will impact the agency’s purchasing program. 
  • Gathers and analyzes data and determines the best procurement method based on State policy to make socially-responsible purchases. 
  • Sources new suppliers and projects for Office’s future needs. 
  • Develops and oversees the competitive bidding process, including Request for Proposals (RFP), Request for Offers (RFO) and Invitation for Bids (IFB).
  • Receives items, manages inventory and coordinates with vendors to facilitate adjustments and/or returns.
  • Makes and implements recommendations for process improvement.
  • Approves purchase orders in FI$Cal. 
  • Processes receipts in FI$Cal and works with DGS Contracted Fiscal Services (CFS) to ensure that invoices are paid timely. 
  • Coordinates and directs DGS CFS on Accounts Receivable billing for services rendered. 
  • Analyzes financial and procurement data. 
  • Tracks contractor payments to ensure they meet the contract requirements. Approves invoices.
  • Supervises, trains, evaluates, and assists subordinate staff.
  • Assigns and evaluates workload and assigns special projects to staff.

About ODI

We deliver better services to Californians through technology and design. We do so through close partnership with other state departments. Our goal is to create programs and products that are more equitable and effective. We listen to Californians who use a program now or may do so tomorrow and hear about what they need.

Diversity, equity, and inclusion at ODI
