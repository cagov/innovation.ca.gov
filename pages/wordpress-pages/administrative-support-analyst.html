We are looking for two Administrative Support Analysts to join our Office of Digital Innovation (ODI) team. This role performs duties as necessary to provide for effective delivery of department services by relieving the Deputy Directors and division leadership of administrative tasks. The Administrative Support Analysts perform strategic and high-level administrative work that will have a large impact to the ODI team.

You will own the processes that ensure thorough and accurate information is provided to the Deputy Director. You will also strategically manage leadership schedules and involvement in projects and decision-making processes, ensuring appropriate balance of attention to highest priority items. In addition, you will review and analyze proposed policy, procedures, and program changes, prepare reports, interpret department and institution policies to operating programs, and make recommendations.  

The duties of the Administrative Support Analysts are of a complex and varied nature, involving the use of independent judgment, evaluating complex situations and taking appropriate action. You will anticipate the needs of others in order to ensure a seamless and positive experience. Most importantly, you will help us in our mission to deliver better services to the people of California.

Location: ODI staff reside throughout California. This position provides telework opportunities in accordance with agency telework policies. (Must currently reside in California)

Annual Salary: $64,596 to $80,868 based on experience

Benefits: The state offers many benefits

What you’ll do

  • Independently provides support to ODI’s leadership team to provide relief of administrative detail; make recommendations on a broad spectrum of administrative and program-related problems within ODI; respond to public inquiries related to ODI; research and prepare correspondence and reports; develop and execute communication plans for ODI and Leadership Team as needed; write unit procedures and correspondence as necessary to fully address ODI related functions.
  • Review and analyze proposed policy, procedures, and program changes, prepare reports, interpret Department policies to operating programs, and make recommendations to the leadership team.
  • Scheduling meetings, drafting documents and preparing special events. 
  • Independently conducts research on leadership’s initiatives, policies, best practices, and other matters on behalf of the Deputy Directors and other managers. 
  • Keeps executives informed on the status of issues and assignments.
  • Researches and secures information to respond to complex requests.
  • Cooperates with other agencies, groups, and individuals in connection with the coordination of departmental activities.
  • Reviews, researches and draft responses to a variety of correspondence and communications that are routed to the leadership team.

About ODI

We deliver better services to Californians through technology and design. We do so through close partnership with other state departments. Our goal is to create programs and products that are more equitable and effective. We listen to Californians who use a program now or may do so tomorrow and hear about what they need.

We:

  • Build tools and products
  • Create and enhance digital services
  • Conduct research
  • Identify data solutions
  • Offer training

We are a cross-discipline staff with blended backgrounds in:

  • Design
  • Engineering
  • Product
  • Operations
  • Data
  • Research
  • Policy
  • Strategy

Diversity, equity, and inclusion at ODI

Diversity, equity, and inclusion help us deliver better government services to Californians.

  • Fostering diversity inspires better ideas and more innovative thinking.
  • Feeling included nurtures a shared sense of community. This drives us to take collective action on challenging problems.
  • Prioritizing equity ensures we build solutions for people whose needs are often overlooked.

We acknowledge our history and present of implicit and structural injustice, bias, and discrimination. We see, hear, and accept each other and the lived experiences we bring to our work. We support each other in learning and developing so we can help all Californians thrive.

We understand this work is never done. That’s why we commit to constant improvement.
