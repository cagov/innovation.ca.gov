We’re looking for two Procurement & Contracts Analysts to analyze, implement, and monitor complex information technology (IT) and non-IT goods and services, purchases, and contracts for the Office of Digital Innovation (ODI). As a Procurement & Contracts Analyst, you will support improving fiscal, budgetary, and administrative practices to deliver transparency, risk minimization, and better financial strategies for ODI. You have strong analytical skills and can share your analysis and recommendations in an easy-to-follow format. You also have strong interpersonal skills and are good at building relationships and educating others on processes, policies, and best practices related to financial administration. You love collaborating with others and are open to feedback, always looking for ways to improve current processes and bring creative solutions to the table.

Location: ODI staff reside throughout California. This position provides telework opportunities in accordance with agency telework policies. (Must currently reside in California)

Annual Salary: $64,596 to $80,868 based on experience

Benefits: The state offers many benefits

What you’ll do

  • Interpret and apply state and departmental policy to ensure state procurement practices and rules are followed and appropriately documented for ODI. 
  • Responsible for analyzing and processing a wide range of requisitions for IT and non-IT goods and services, including but not limited to: Request for Proposals, Invitation for Bids, Leveraged Procurement Agreement, California Multiple Award Schedules, Statewide contracts, Master Services Agreements, Software License Programs, Cooperative Agreements, Small Business (SB)/ Disabled Veterans Business Enterprise (DVBE) Option, and Non-Competitive Bid Justifications. 
  • Attend meetings with department staff to provide guidance on state purchasing policies and procedures; provide technical advice and consulting on topics such as solicitation development and procurement types/methods; assists in providing evaluation criteria for the appropriate use of Leveraged Procurement Agreements and required justifications. 
  • Assist in the preparation and evaluation of solicitations, requisitions, and purchase orders. Review documents, preliminary solicitations, purchase requisitions, required forms and correspondences for correctness, errors, and form consistency. 
  • Communicate with vendors to obtain price quotes, DGS solicitation forms such as STD204 and STD205, invoices, and other required documentation.
  • Coordinate purchasing-related reports, including CalRecycle and FI$Cal modules for DGS and other special ad hoc statistical reports as needed.
  • Coordinate the collection of statistical data, documents, and reports.
  • Maintain electronic procurement files.
  • Process invoices, direct transfers, and lease payments. Prepare Payment Approval Labels, invoice documents, and route invoice packages to the State Controller’s Office and DGS Contracted Fiscal Services. Track payments in Fi$Cal to ensure they are received by vendors.
  • Record Contract, Invoice, and Purchase order details in ODI’s internal tracking system.
  • Reconcile Procurement Card purchases monthly. 
  • Creates, tracks and analyzes expenses on the agency’s Cal-Cards accounts. Coordinates with the Agency on Cal-Card payments.
  • Run reports and queries in Fi$Cal upon request by ODI staff and management to gather the data needed to monitor program contract expenditures.

About ODI

We deliver better services to Californians through technology and design. We do so through close partnership with other state departments. Our goal is to create programs and products that are more equitable and effective. We listen to Californians who use a program now or may do so tomorrow and hear about what they need.

We:
