Are you passionate about technical project management? ODI is looking for a project manager to oversee critical projects and initiatives. You will be responsible for planning, organizing, and directing projects, ensuring project deliverables, managing resources, and overseeing communication with stakeholders, the team, executive management and control agencies.

Location: ODI staff reside throughout California. This position provides telework opportunities in accordance with agency telework policies. ODI is currently operating in a hybrid work environment with staff reporting to the office 2 days a week to either Sacramento HQ or our Oakland Hub.  

Annual salary: $100,488 – $134,640 

Salary information: CalHR salary rules allow appointment at the entrance rate (Cal. Code Regs., tit. 2, § 599.673) of a classification. For classes with alternate ranges, placement is based on education/experience.

Benefits: The state offers many benefits, including pension, health benefits (including after retirement), life insurance, employee assistance program, and much more.

Last day to apply: Final filing date is 2/18/2025.

What you’ll do

  • Assists with development of ODI’s project governance framework.
  • Oversees and manages scope, resources, and schedules for all aspects related to assigned projects.
  • Plans, implements, and coordinates project activities with internal and/or external stakeholders oversight agencies and vendor teams.
  • Participates in and oversees the planning, development, implementation, and maintenance of individual project objectives and short- and long-range plans; develops tracking and evaluation programs to assist in the accomplishment of established project goals and objectives.
  • Manages the working relationships and collaboration between project leadership, agency, department directorate, business partners, and stakeholders.

Desirable qualifications

In addition to evaluating each candidate’s relative ability, as demonstrated by quality and breadth of experience, the following factors will provide the basis for competitively evaluating each candidate:

  • Knowledge of industry-standard project tracking software
  • Experience creating communication plans and processes
  • Experience developing plans to mitigate risks
  • Experience working successfully with cross-functional teams
  • Experience maintaining project time frames, budget estimates and status reports
  • Experience defining project responsibilities with stakeholders
  • Experience ensuring all activities are performed in a manner consistent with documented processes
  • Demonstrated ability to fully understand the life cycle of the project
  • Ability to analyze and develop procedures for management and technical duties
  • Ability to participate in project budget and staff allocation
  • Ability to work as part of the Project Team, development of scope and schedule for project work
  • Ability to ensure client satisfaction throughout the project process.
  • Ability to ensure compliance with all key performance indicators

About ODI

We deliver better services to Californians through technology and design. We do so through close partnership with other state departments. Our goal is to create programs and products that are more equitable and effective. We listen to Californians who use a program now or may do so tomorrow and learn about what they need.

We:

  • Build tools and products
  • Create and enhance digital services
  • Conduct research

  • Offer training

We are a cross-discipline staff with blended backgrounds in:

  • Product
  • Operations
  • Data
  • Research
  • Policy
  • Strategy

Diversity, equity, and inclusion at ODI

Diversity, equity, and inclusion help us deliver better government services to Californians.
