We’re looking for an experienced Content Strategist to join the team. You will be responsible for the creation and maintenance of clear and accessible content of varying types and on varying channels for the Office of Digital Innovation (ODI) and our agency partners. You will collaborate with agency partners, product managers, other designers, and engineers to deliver user-centered content for audiences both inside and outside of government. You will work closely with our partners to improve or develop their user-centered content practices and promote content strategy and best practices across government. The Content Strategist will play a major role in advising management or formulating information technology strategy and policy within the organization.

Location: ODI staff reside throughout California. This position provides telework opportunities in accordance with agency telework policies. (Must currently reside in California)

Annual Salary: $92,400 to $123,816 based on experience and qualifications

Benefits: The state offers many benefits

What you’ll do

  • Executes the content creation process from start to finish, including but not limited to: planning, writing, editing and testing clear, accessible, and concise content in a fast-paced, collaborative environment. Content types may include  landing pages, interface writing, navigation labels, notifications, research findings, documentation, and release notes.
  • Contributes to increasing the quality and visibility of relevant content and services on state websites by applying best practices in search engine optimization (SEO) in search engine results (i.e. edit content, add content, and modify HTML and associated coding) to both increase its relevance to specific keywords and remove barriers to the indexing activities of search engines.
  • Redesigns and creates new content based on research, analytics, and program or product expertise.
  • Translates user experience (UX) research into language that anticipates needs and streamlines the user experience.
  • Plans, organizes, directs and edits content for editorial users and audiences.
  • Develops the voice and tone of the digital products through writing and research.
  • Participates in creating the design of new content system architecture, standards, and methods to support organizational needs by implementing modern web content design styles and standards.
  • Collaborates with the engineering team and designers to create product features that amplify content and user needs.
  • Conducts content audits and analyzes reader feedback to evaluate content effectiveness.
  • Conducts and analyzes user research to gain a deep understanding of user needs to identify how content can improve their interaction with the state. 
  • Troubleshoots, tracks, and conducts root cause analysis of system, database, and operational issues in the area of content design to understand where content improvements can enhance the user experience.
  • Collaborates closely with partners and subject matter experts to increase the visibility of state websites or web pages and establish a shared vision for the user experience.
  • Supports and scales ODI’s content design function and improve competencies of other state teams through playbooks, approaches, communities of practice and other training channels.

About ODI

We deliver better services to Californians through technology and design. We do so through close partnership with other state departments. Our goal is to create programs and products that are more equitable and effective. We listen to Californians who use a program now or may do so tomorrow and hear about what they need.

We:

  • Build tools and products
  • Create and enhance digital services
  • Conduct research
  • Identify data solutions
  • Offer training

We are a cross-discipline staff with blended backgrounds in:

  • Design
  • Engineering
  • Product
  • Operations
  • Data
  • Research
  • Policy
  • Strategy

Diversity, equity, and inclusion at ODI

Diversity, equity, and inclusion help us deliver better government services to Californians.

  • Fostering diversity inspires better ideas and more innovative thinking.
  • Feeling included nurtures a shared sense of community. This drives us to take collective action on challenging problems.
  • Prioritizing equity ensures we build solutions for people whose needs are often overlooked.

We acknowledge our history and present of implicit and structural injustice, bias, and discrimination. We see, hear, and accept each other and the lived experiences we bring to our work. We support each other in learning and developing so we can help all Californians thrive.

We understand this work is never done. That’s why we commit to constant improvement.
